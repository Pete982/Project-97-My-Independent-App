Basketball Facts

Farthest Shot Made in Basketball

The farthest shot is 89 feet. You May Think that Steph Curry made that shot, but no, he made a shot from 38 feet. That's not even close to the farthest ever shot in Basketball History. In 2001, Baron Davis made a shot from 89 feet away! The average basketball court is 91 feet long. He basically shot from the beginning of the court to its end.

Tallest Basketball Players

Basketball is known for having tall fans and players. Manute Bol and Gheorghe Muresan both stand at a towering height of 7 foot 7 and are part of the 2,800 people in the world that are 7 feet tall or heigher. That's roughly about 0.000038% percent of the population.

Dribbling Wasn't Allowed

The first team credited with moving the ball down the court by dribbling it played at Yale in 1897. After this move was recognized and learned about, a new rule materialized. The rule was, "a player can only dribble once before passing the ball. Unless the ball is returned back to that player, they cannot shoot or do anything but go for the ball or guard their man". After four years time, dribbling a basketball became a quaterstone of the sport. Dissapointingly, another important basketball move, the slam dunk, was banned just before the 1967-1968 season. After some time, it was unbanned in the 1976-1977 season. A lot of changes have happened to Basketball in the last 55 or so years.

Basketball Achievements

Greatest Trophy Received
Amazing Trophy Recieved by Larry O'Brien.

The Larry O'Brien NBA Championship Trophy is the championship trophy awarded annually by the National Basketball Association (NBA) to the winner of the NBA Finals.

The Towering Score of the Most Point Scoring Team.
Highest Score in a Single Basketball Game

In 1983, the Detroit Pistons beat the Denver Nuggets at a spectacular score of 186 to 184 points in triple overtime. It was, and still is the highest scoring game in NBA's history.

The longest game played in the NBA
Longest Lasting Game

The longest NBA basketball game is 78 minutes and was played between the Indianapolis Olympians and Rochester Royals (both USA). It was played at Edgerton Park Arena, in Rochester, New York, USA, on January 6th 1951. The game was won by the Olympians 75 to 73 in what turned out to be a six-overtime game.
